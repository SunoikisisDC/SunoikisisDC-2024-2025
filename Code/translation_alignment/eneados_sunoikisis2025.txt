Virgil, Aeneid, I.1-
Gavin Douglas, Eneados

Latin:

I.1. Arma virumque cano troiae qui primus ab oris
I.2. Italiam fato profugus lauiniaque venit
I.3. Litora: multum ille et terris iactatus et alto
I.4. Vi superum saeuae memorem iunonis ob iram:
I.5. Multa quoque et bello passus: dum conderet vrbem:
I.6. inferretque deos latio: genus unde latinum:
I.7. Albanique patres atque altae moenia romae.

Scots:

I.i.a. The poet first proponyng his entent 
I.i.b. Declaris Iunois wreth and mailtalent. 
I.i.1. The batalis and the man I wil discrive
I.i.2. Fra Troyis boundis first that fugitive 
I.i.3. By fait to Ytail come and cost Lavyne,
I.i.4. Our land and sey katchit with mekil pyne 
I.i.5. By forss of goddis abufe, from euery steid, 
I.i.6. Of cruell Iuno throu ald remembrit fede. 
I.i.7. Gret pane in batail sufferit he alsso 
I.i.8. Or he his goddis brocht in Latio
I.i.9. And belt the cite fra quham, of nobill fame, 
I.i.10. The Latyne pepill takyn heth thar name, 
I.i.11. And eik the faderis, princis of Alba,
I.i.12. Cam, and the wallaris of gret Rome alswa.
